Night City Derby: Neo-France Outsider Takes the Gold Cup — Then the Beachfront Oysters Take Their Revenge

In what racing commentators are already calling the upset of the decade, Geoffroi Deschandeliers of Neo-France crossed the finish line at the Night City Derby today aboard Bottle of Smoke — the only horse in the field running without Biotechnica corporate sponsorship — to claim the Gold Cup by a length and a half over the heavily favored Biotechnica-backed Helix Prime.

The crowd at Pacifica Downs went silent as Bottle of Smoke cleared the final straight, then erupted. Six Biotechnica-sponsored mounts, each outfitted with the company's proprietary BioStride performance augmentation program, were left trailing. Biotechnica's racing division has not yet issued a statement, though several track-side reps were observed leaving early.

Geoffroi Deschandeliers, 29, wept openly on the saddle before the trophy presentation, crediting his trainer and what he described as "a horse that simply wanted it more." He dedicated the win to his home circuit in Lyon. The Gold Cup — a two-kilogram solid chrome trophy shaped in the likeness of a Night City skyline — was presented by Derby Chairwoman Lena Cho under heavy media flash.

The celebration was cut short, however, when Geoffroi was seen doubled over behind the podium approximately twenty minutes after the ceremony. Witnesses confirm the rider had spent the morning hours sampling raw oysters from a vendor operating on the Night City beachfront, a stretch of coastline that environmental monitors have flagged no fewer than four times this year for elevated chemical runoff from the Petrochem refinery corridor.

The Night City Lifeguard Association (NCLA) confirmed late this afternoon that warning signs have been posted along the affected beachfront stretch and that a small number of squatters occupying the dunes near the vendor stalls have been relocated pending a coastal safety assessment. "We strongly advise against consuming any raw or unprocessed seafood sourced from Night City coastal waters," said NCLA spokesman Dario Muñoz in a brief release. "This should not require a champion jockey to illustrate."

Geoffroi was treated on-site by medical staff and is reported to be recovering. He is expected to make the flight back to Neo-France with the Gold Cup tomorrow morning.
- Naomi Wu, 6/26/2045

NetWatch Issues Level-3 Blackwall Warning After Massive Data Spike Detected Over Watson:

NetWatch has elevated its subnet alert status to Level-3 following an anomalous data spike originating from the Watson district late Wednesday evening. Analysts describe the signal pattern as consistent with Rogue AI activity probing the Blackwall perimeter — the hardened firewall separating the public Net from the digital wasteland left behind by the DataKrash of '09.

NetWatch field teams were deployed to Watson within the hour, locking down several public terminals and severing two local access nodes as a precautionary measure. Residents in the area reported brief but total loss of braindance feeds and AR overlays during the blackout window.

"We neutralized the intrusion vector before any breach occurred," said NetWatch spokesperson Hana Yee in a brief statement. "The public Net remains secure." Critics were quick to point out that NetWatch has issued similar assurances twice in the past year, both times preceding confirmed Rogue AI incursions elsewhere in the city.

NCPD has declined to comment. Watson locals are advised to report any unusual cyberware behavior or unexplained neural feedback to the nearest precinct.
- Naomi Wu, 6/25/2045

Militech Arms Cache Raided in Pacifica — Corporate Security Suspects Inside Job:

A Militech logistics depot operating under civilian cover in the Pacifica district was stripped of an estimated 4.2 million eurodollars in military-grade hardware early Tuesday morning. Sources close to the investigation describe the missing inventory as including Smart-linked assault platforms, encrypted comm arrays, and at least one crate of experimental non-lethal deterrent systems currently under NUSA evaluation contract.

What makes the breach remarkable is what was left behind. Security footage — what little wasn't wiped — shows the raid conducted in under eleven minutes, with no forced entry at the primary access points. Internal badge logs place three Militech contractors at the facility during the theft window, all of whom have since gone dark.

"Militech takes the security of its assets extremely seriously and is cooperating fully with an internal investigation," read a terse corporate statement released Thursday. No mention of NCPD involvement was made.

Street-level chatter in Pacifica suggests the hardware has already begun moving through fixer networks. Militech has quietly put a bounty on the contractors. No figure has been confirmed.
- Duke Raoul, 6/22/2045
